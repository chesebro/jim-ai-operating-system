# Boris Cherny — AI-Native PM and GTM Thinking

**Source:** Lenny’s Podcast  
**Topic:** Claude Code, AI-native product design  
**Relevance:** Core shift in how products are built, adopted, and defended in the AI era

---

# Core Shift

AI changes product management from feature design into workflow and system design.

You are not building features.

You are designing:
- workflows
- delegation systems
- learning loops
- compounding advantage

---

# Key Product Ideas

## 1. Workflow > Interface

The product is where it lives in the user’s work, not how it looks.

Focus:
- what work is removed
- what workflow is owned

---

## 2. Latent Demand > Stated Demand

Users rarely ask for what they truly want.

Look for:
- repeated manual stitching
- re-explaining the same context
- procrastinated work
- broken workflows

---

## 3. Trust > Time Savings

Strong products increase confidence, not just speed.

Measure:
- more ambitious attempts
- better decisions
- completed workflows

---

## 4. Abundance > Efficiency

AI enables exploration.

Allow:
- more attempts
- more drafts
- more parallel paths

Optimize for outcomes, not minimal usage.

---

## 5. System Behavior > Features

Moat comes from:
- workflow embed
- feedback loops
- context accumulation
- trust
- adoption

Not from features.

---

## 6. Data as Moat (Conditional)

Data compounds when it is:
- proprietary
- longitudinal
- embedded in workflow
- tied to outcomes
- hard to recreate

Loop:
usage → data → better output → trust → more usage

---

## 7. Delegation Spectrum

Products evolve across:

- Assist → Draft → Execute → Monitor

Staying at assist limits value.

---

## 8. Artifact Design

Strong systems produce persistent artifacts:
- plans
- models
- memory
- workflows

These improve over time.

---

## 9. Adoption is Behavioral

Users don’t need training.

They need:
- use case discovery
- confidence
- small wins

---

# GTM Implications

## Start with Workflow

Not personas.

Define:
- painful, repeated work
- where cost or friction exists

---

## Trigger > Value Prop

People act when something breaks:
- cost
- risk
- complexity
- uncertainty

---

## Sell Work Removed

Do not describe features.

Describe:
- what disappears
- what becomes automatic

---

## Product and GTM Are Linked

Your wedge determines:
- who adopts
- how it spreads
- what data you get

---

# Five PM Rules

1. Design for workflows, not features  
2. Prioritize latent demand  
3. Optimize for trust and confidence  
4. Build systems that learn  
5. Separate assist, recommend, and act
