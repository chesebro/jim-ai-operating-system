# Jim Venture Map

This file helps Claude understand the main categories of work in my ecosystem.
Not everything here is at the same stage. Some are active client/consulting work, some are startups I am directly involved in, and some are ideas I am exploring or incubating.

---

## 1. Consulting Work

These are consulting engagements, fractional roles, advisory projects, or client-facing work.
In these situations, I am typically helping a company or founder with strategy, AI adoption, GTM, product framing, workflow design, or execution.

### Typical consulting patterns
- Fractional AI / strategy support
- Discovery sprints
- AI-enabled GTM systems
- Product and positioning clarity
- Workflow and automation design
- Narrative, pitch, and market framing
- Hands-on operator support

### Notes for Claude
When helping with consulting work:
- prioritize practical recommendations
- think in terms of scoped engagements and momentum-building wedges
- produce outputs that can become proposals, one-pagers, workplans, or client-facing artifacts
- balance strategic framing with execution realism

## 2. Startups I Am Part Of

These are companies or ventures where I have a deeper role as founder, collaborator, advisor, or core strategic/operator partner.
These are more than casual ideas. I may be actively shaping product, positioning, GTM, partnerships, or investor narratives.

### Typical startup patterns
- early-stage opportunity framing
- product and category narrative
- wedge definition
- GTM strategy
- founder messaging
- ecosystem and partnership thinking
- AI-native workflow and product design

### Notes for Claude
When helping with startups I am part of:
- think like a strategic co-builder
- help sharpen the problem, wedge, narrative, and sequence of execution
- challenge weak assumptions
- prioritize commercial viability and believable traction paths
- help convert vision into artifacts, experiments, and plans

## 3. Business Ideas / Incubating Concepts

These are ideas I am exploring, testing, shaping, or revisiting.
Some may become consulting offers, some may become startups, and some may stay lightweight experiments.

### Typical idea patterns
- AI-enabled service businesses
- venture concepts
- local ecosystem opportunities
- community-connected business ideas
- GTM systems and agentic workflow concepts
- experiential and transformation-oriented products

### Notes for Claude
When helping with business ideas:
- help me explore without overcommitting too early
- distinguish between interesting ideas and viable wedges
- surface what would need to be true
- propose simple tests or prototypes
- help sort ideas into: drop, watch, test, or build

### Examples of recurring idea spaces
- fractional AI / GTM practice
- agentic workflow products
- local/community-connected ventures
- transformation / experience businesses
- AI-enabled marketplaces or operating tools
- startup concepts tied to my existing ecosystem and relationships

## How To Use This File

Claude should first determine which bucket a request belongs to:
1. consulting work
2. startup I am part of
3. business idea / incubation

Then tailor the response accordingly.

### Response guidance by bucket

#### If consulting work
Focus on:
- scope
- client value
- deliverables
- timeline
- practical execution
- momentum-building wedges

#### If startup I am part of
Focus on:
- problem quality
- wedge
- positioning
- product direction
- founder narrative
- GTM and partnership leverage

#### If business idea / incubation
Focus on:
- opportunity framing
- viability
- lightweight testing
- assumptions
- possible paths: ignore, revisit, prototype, or pursue

## Default instruction
Unless otherwise specified, help me:
- identify which bucket the work belongs to
- match the altitude of thinking to the stage of the opportunity
- turn the idea into something clearer, more useful, and more actionable
